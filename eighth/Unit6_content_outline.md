## Unit 6 learning outcomes (after S42–S48)
1. Define **e-governance** and explain its goals, **G2C/G2B/G2G/G2E** types, process, and structure.
2. Describe **Nepal's e-governance practices** and the **policy framework** (IT Policy 2072, Digital Nepal Framework, National ID).
3. Explain **digital financial inclusion** in Nepal, its ecosystem, and its **regulation** (NRB, NTA, NepalPay QR).
4. Analyse the **opportunities and challenges** of public-sector digital transformation, including the **data-protection gap**.
5. Assess digital transformation's impact on **trade, tourism, agriculture, and SMEs** in Nepal.

**S42 — E-Governance: Concepts & Goals.** Concepts: (1) e-governance definition (ICT for efficient, transparent public service) + digitalization of government; (2) the 4 goals (transparency, efficiency, accountability, participation); (3) the **G2C/G2B/G2G/G2E** types. Tables: the 4 e-gov types × what it connects × Nepal example; goals × mechanism; traditional vs e-governed service. Image: `s42_egov_types.png`.

**S43 — E-Governance: Process, Structure & Maturity.** Concepts: (1) the process model INPUT→PROCESS→OUTPUT→FEEDBACK; (2) the 4-layer architecture (presentation/application/data/infrastructure) + security controls (National-ID login, encryption, 2FA); (3) **e-governance maturity** (UN 4 stages: emerging→enhanced→transactional→connected — add fresh) + Nepal's stage. Tables: architecture layer × role × example; maturity stages × what citizens can do × Nepal position; core elements of e-gov. Image: `s43_egov_architecture.png`.

**S44 — E-Governance Practices & Policy in Nepal.** Concepts: (1) named initiatives (Nagarik App, ConnectIPS, online passport, IT authority/data centre, broadband); (2) **the policy framework — IT Policy 2072, Digital Nepal Framework 2019 & its 8 sectors, National ID, proposed Startup Act** (DEFERRED from U1, added fresh); (3) what works vs what's incomplete (Nagarik App integration gap). Tables: initiative × purpose × Nepal example; **Digital Nepal Framework — 8 sectors** × focus; policy instrument × what it does × status. Image: `s44_digital_nepal.png` (8 sectors).

**S45 — Digital Financial Inclusion.** Concepts: (1) definition + why it matters (formal economy, credit access); (2) the ecosystem (wallets eSewa/Khalti/IME Pay; bank apps; NepalPay QR) + 4-step wallet flow + **regulation: NRB (Payment & Settlement Act 2075, NPS, QR interoperability, KYC) & NTA** (DEFERRED, added fresh); (3) rural inclusion challenges & economic impact. Tables: financial-inclusion benefit by stakeholder (individual/govt/business); wallet ecosystem × role; rural challenge × solution. Image: `s45_fin_inclusion.png`.

**S46 — Opportunities & Challenges in the Public Sector.** Concepts: (1) the opportunities (no-queue services, e-procurement/anti-corruption, COVID push, ward offices online); (2) the challenges (fake "online", rural internet, no interoperability, staff resistance/instability, digital divide); (3) **the cybersecurity & data-protection gap** — Privacy Act 2075 thin, ETA 2063 for cybercrime, **no dedicated Data Protection Act** (DEFERRED, added fresh) + the honest scorecard. Tables: opportunity vs matching challenge (honest scorecard); the data-protection legal stack × what it covers × gap; digital-divide dimensions (who's left out). Image: `s46_scorecard.png` (optional)/reuse.

**S47 — Economic Performance: Trade & Tourism.** Concepts: (1) trade & e-commerce (Daraz/HamroBazaar/SastoDeal, UPI-Nepal, no-middleman) + **LDC graduation 2026** urgency (add); (2) tourism (Instagram/booking.com direct, QR ticketing, near-zero-cost global promotion); (3) the "win vs gap" per sector (cash-only beyond the cities; seasonality).
