## Unit 4 learning outcomes (after S26–S33)
1. Define **digital transformation** and distinguish **digitization / digitalization / DT**.
2. Identify the **drivers** of digital transformation (technological, economic, social, + crisis).
3. Explain how DT **accelerates the SDGs** (esp. SDG 4, 8, 9, 16) and what ICT4D means.
4. Explain the **technological revolution's role in the world economy** (productivity, platform economy, labor).
5. Distinguish **traditional vs digital globalization** and explain digital-age **economic growth**.
6. Define **digital currencies**, distinguish **digital money vs digital currency**, and compare **crypto / stablecoin / CBDC** — including Nepal's ban.

## Sessions (concepts → §7A tables → image)

**S26 — DT: Concepts (digitization → digitalization → transformation).** Concepts: (1) what DT is (change how orgs operate & deliver value — processes, models, culture); (2) the 3-tier distinction (digitization=data, digitalization=process, DT=business model); (3) why DT is survival (Kodak/Nokia failures). Tables: digitization vs digitalization vs DT (comparison); before→after by domain (examples ≥6); DT-or-die cases. Image: `s26_three_tier.png`.

**S27 — Drivers of Digital Transformation.** Concepts: (1) technological drivers (internet/mobile/cloud/big data/AI/IoT); (2) economic + social drivers (cost/competition/productivity; changing behaviour, policy); (3) crisis as accelerator (COVID-19). Tables: 4 driver categories × Nepal example; COVID before/after adoption; driver → business impact. Image: `s27_drivers.png`.

**S28 — Accelerating SDGs through Digital Transformation.** Concepts: (1) SDGs & why DT accelerates them (5 mechanisms: speed/reach/efficiency/transparency/scalability); (2) the four mapped SDGs (4 education, 8 work, 9 innovation, 16 governance); (3) ICT4D + the digital-divide challenge. Tables: SDG × DT mechanism × Nepal example; ICT4D focus areas; DT-for-SDG benefits vs challenges. Image: `s28_sdg.png`.

**S29 — Technological Revolution & the World Economy.** (4IR timeline already in U1 S6 — cross-ref.) Concepts: (1) the revolution→economy link (productivity, new industries, disruption of old); (2) the platform economy & global supply chains (network effects, transaction cost — link U2); (3) labor-market shift (jobs vanish/appear, gig, digital skills). Tables: jobs disappearing vs appearing (examples); sector-wise Nepal impact; traditional vs digital-economy firm. Image: `s29_disruption.png` (optional) / reuse.

**S30 — Globalization in the Digital Age.** Concepts: (1) globalization + traditional vs digital globalization; (2) e-commerce (B2C/B2B/C2C) & digital trade (services/products/data flow); (3) opportunities & risks for developing countries + Nepal's position (freelancing, IT export, "skills + internet, no factory"). Tables: traditional vs digital globalization (comparison); e-commerce types; opportunity vs risk for Nepal. Image: `s30_globalization.png`.

**S31 — Digital Transformation & Economic Growth.** Concepts: (1) digital economy & GDP (skills→jobs→income→GDP); (2) growth engines — **low marginal cost + network effects** (link U2), innovation, digital finance; (3) government's role (Digital Nepal, infrastructure) & the digital divide. Tables: growth engine × how it works × Nepal example; digital infrastructure components; who benefits vs digital divide. Image: `s31_growth_flow.png`.

**S32 — Digital Currencies: Concepts.** Concepts: (1) evolution of money (barter→coins→paper→bank→digital); (2) what a digital currency is (electronic-only, wallet, network) + physical vs digital money; (3) **digital money ≠ digital currency** (eSewa = NRB-issued NPR in electronic form, not a currency creator). Tables: physical vs digital money (comparison); eSewa money vs cryptocurrency (comparison); why-it-emerged (scaffolding). Image: `s32_money_evolution.png`.

**S33 — Digital Currencies: Types & Future (closes unit).** Concepts: (1) cryptocurrency (decentralised, blockchain, volatile) + **Nepal's ban** (NRB, reasons + legal basis); (2) stablecoin (pegged, remittance use) & CBDC (central-bank digital money — Digital Yuan/eNaira/India pilot; NRB study status); (3) regulation approaches & the future (cash decline, tokenization). Tables: crypto vs stablecoin vs CBDC (comparison); crypto advantages vs risks; regulatory approaches (ban/regulate/sandbox/CBDC). Image: `s33_currency_types.png`.
