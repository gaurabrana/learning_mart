## Unit 5 learning outcomes (after S34–S41)
1. Explain **information as an economic good** and distinguish **symmetric vs asymmetric** information.
2. Explain **adverse selection** and **moral hazard** (before/after the deal), the **market for lemons**, and the determinants of asymmetry.
3. Explain how **digitalization, signaling & screening** reduce asymmetry — and the **new** asymmetries digital markets create.
4. Explain how **online search engines** and **AI** reshape information economics (search cost, market efficiency, and new power).
5. Apply the **new economics of information** to strategy (versioning, price discrimination, lock-in, bundling).
6. Analyse digitalization's effects on **consumer choice** and **labor markets**.
7. Explain **intellectual property** types and how **digitalization** challenges them.

## Sessions (concepts → §7A tables → image)

**S34 — Foundations: Information as an Economic Good.** Concepts: (1) information as an economic good (non-rival, reusable, cheap to copy) vs physical goods; (2) symmetric vs asymmetric information; (3) perfect vs imperfect information & why info is costly. Tables: physical good vs information (comparison); symmetric vs asymmetric (examples ≥6); determinants of information *quality*. Image: `s34_info_good.png` (physical vs information good).

**S35 — Asymmetric Information: Lemons, Adverse Selection & Moral Hazard.** Concepts: (1) adverse selection (before the deal) + **the market for lemons, worked** (average-price cascade — build the mechanism); (2) moral hazard (after the deal); (3) the 5 determinants of asymmetry. Tables: adverse selection vs moral hazard (comparison); the lemons cascade (scaffolding); 5 determinants × Nepal example. Image: `s35_lemons.png` (adverse-selection cascade).

**S36 — Reducing Asymmetry: Signaling, Screening & Digitalization.** Concepts: (1) signaling (informed side proves quality — warranties, certification, education, brand); (2) screening (uninformed side sorts — insurance forms, test drives, reviews); (3) digitalization as a gap-reducer AND new-asymmetry-creator (fake reviews, sponsored rank). Tables: signaling vs screening (comparison, who acts); reduce-asymmetry mechanisms × Nepal example; old vs digital used-car market. Image: `s36_signal_screen.png`.

**S37 — Online Search Engines & the Economics of Search.** Concepts: (1) search cost & price dispersion (search engines shrink both); (2) comparison markets & near-"fair value" pricing; (3) the search engine as a NEW gatekeeper (SEO, paid ranking, "best" = most promoted). Tables: before vs after search engines; how search reduces cost (scaffolding); genuine vs paid/ranked result (examples). Image: none (tables) / reuse.

**S38 — Artificial Intelligence & Information.** Concepts: (1) AI reduces asymmetry (fraud detection, verified reviews, transparency); (2) recommendation engines & personalization (market efficiency); (3) AI's limits & ethics (bias, privacy, explainability, black-box). Tables: how AI changes information economics (scaffolding); AI benefit vs risk by stakeholder; consumer/business/labor impact. Image: `s38_ai_info.png` (AI shrinking the info gap) — optional.

**S39 — Strategy & the New Economics of Information.** Concepts: (1) the shift: information scarce→abundant changes strategy; (2) the toolkit — **versioning & price discrimination, bundling** (sell the same info many ways); (3) **lock-in, switching costs & network effects** as info-strategy (link Unit 2) + AI-driven dynamic pricing. Tables: old vs new economics of information; strategy tool × how it monetises info × example; versioning example (scaffolding). Image: none / reuse.

**S40 — Digitalization: Consumer Choice & Labor Markets.** Concepts: (1) consumer choice — lower search cost, information overload, bounded rationality, e-WOM; (2) algorithms & who controls choice; (3) labor markets — displacement vs creation, **polarization** (middle-skill shrinks), gig economy & algorithmic management, reskilling. Tables: before vs after digital consumer choice; jobs displaced vs created (examples ≥6); gig-work benefit vs cost. Image: `s40_polarization.png` (labor-market polarization).

**S41 — Intellectual Property & Digitalization (closes unit).** Concepts: (1) IP & the four types (copyright, patent, trademark, trade secret) — why IP exists; (2) digitalization's double edge (reach vs piracy) + platform enforcement (Content ID); (3) the AI-ownership problem & Nepal's IP challenge (balance). Tables: the four IP types (comparison, + Nepal example); digital good vs bad for IP; piracy vs fair use vs licensing (examples). Image: `s41_ip_types.png` (four IP types).
